From the founder

Joshua Stanton

Feed The DNA wasn't created because I wanted to start another company. It was created because I believe people are meant to come together.

Over the years I've worked in the trades, served as a first responder, built businesses, organized fundraisers, created media, and raised my daughter. Every part of that journey reinforced the same belief. There are incredible people everywhere doing meaningful work, but too often they're building alone. I kept seeing good people, good businesses, and good ideas that simply weren't connected.

I've always found the most joy in bringing people together. Watching someone meet the right person, discover a new opportunity, grow a business, solve a problem, or build a friendship because two paths crossed through Feed The DNA is what drives me. I believe there are far more good people in the world than we're led to believe, and when they have a place to connect, incredible things begin to happen.

Feed The DNA is my way of creating that place. It's a platform for the people who build instead of complain, who care about their communities, who believe in supporting one another, and who know that lasting change starts close to home. My hope is that this grows into something much bigger than me — a place where people, businesses, creators, schools, nonprofits, and communities can continue building together long after I'm gone.

I don't believe the future is something we wait for.
I believe it's something we build together.

“I don't see Feed The DNA as a brand. I see it as a place where people come together to build stronger communities.”
