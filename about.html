About the ecosystem

Rebuilding community from the inside out.

Feed The DNA exists to rebuild community from the inside out — connecting local businesses, creators, families, schools, and organizations through human-first media, community projects, and sustainable systems of collective abundance.

One ecosystem

Three arms, one mission.

Empower Humanity is the movement. Feed The DNA is the infrastructure. DNActivate is the media arm. Together, they're a single living system for human restoration.

The movement

Empower Humanity

The emotional and philosophical umbrella. It represents the mission to rebuild community, restore human connection, support local people, and create abundance through cooperation. Human-first infrastructure for collective abundance.

The infrastructure

Feed The DNA

The operating system of the movement. Community projects, local business partnerships, fundraising systems, coalition building, sponsorships, events, and education — connecting people into a working network of mutual support.

The media arm

DNActivate

The storytelling, podcast, interview, and documentary branch. It captures real humans — local businesses, creators, musicians, entrepreneurs, and community leaders building something meaningful.

Our mission

Community is not a concept. It is infrastructure. We do not just talk about change — we build systems for it.

Founder · Community architect

Joshua Stanton

Joshua is the founder of Feed The DNA, DNActivate, and the Empower Humanity movement. He's a creator, community builder, father, storyteller, and human-systems thinker based in Idaho.

His work centers on rebuilding trust, strengthening local communities, supporting small businesses, documenting real human stories, and creating systems that help people thrive. His background spans hands-on trade work, media creation, local organizing, community fundraising, podcasting, and business development — alongside lived experience that shaped a deep belief in human potential, redemption, service, and collective abundance.

He doesn't see Feed The DNA as a normal brand. He sees it as a living system for human restoration.
